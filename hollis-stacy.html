Hollis Stacy (Golfer) - On This Day

Profession: Golfer

Biography: Hollis Stacy is an American professional golfer with three wins at the U.S. Women's Open and eighteen LPGA Tour events. She started golfing early and, at age fifteen, became the youngest player to win the U.S. Girls’ Junior Championship.

After turning professional, Stacy won back-to-back victories at the U.S. Women's Open in 1977 and 1978. While her career took a downturn for a while afterwards, she returned to win that same tournament for a third time in 1984.

Born: March 16, 1954
Birthplace: Savannah, Georgia
Age: 70 years old

Generation: Baby Boomer
Chinese Zodiac: Horse
Star Sign: Pisces

Playing Career

  • 1977-07-24 US Open Women's Golf, Hazeltine National GC: Hollis Stacy wins wire-to-wire by 2 strokes from Nancy Lopez
  • 1978-06-04 Canadian Open Golf (Peter Jackson Classic), Lachute GC: JoAnne Carner wins by 8 strokes from Hollis Stacy
  • 1978-07-23 US Open Women's Golf, CC of Indianapolis: Hollis Stacy successfully defends title by 1 stroke from Sally Little and JoAnne Carner
  • 1980-07-13 US Open Women's Golf, Richland CC: Amy Alcott scores convincing 9 stroke win over runner-up Hollis Stacy
  • 1983-07-03 Canadian Open Women's Golf (Peter Jackson Classic), Beaconsfield GC: Hollis Stacy wins by 2 strokes from JoAnn Carner and Alice Miller
  • 1984-07-15 US Open Women's Golf, Salem CC: Hollis Stacy wins her 3rd US title by 1 stroke from Rosie Jones
