Willie Park Sr. (Golfer) - On This Day

Profession: Golfer

Biography: Willie Park Sr. was a Scottish professional golfer and a golf equipment manufacturer. He was best known for winning the first Open Championship in 1860, and for his subsequent victories in 1863, 1866, and 1875. He played a key role in popularizing golf, as well as his contributions as a competitive player.

Park started his career as a caddie before manufacturing golf equipment. He gained prominence through challenge matches, competing against golfers like Old Tom Morris and Allan Robertson. These matches increased the public interest in golf and set the stage for the sport's professional era.

Park's four Open Championship wins are central to his legacy. He claimed victory in the first Open Championship in 1860 and subsequently won in 1863, 1866, and 1875. These achievements underscored his skill and placed him among the sport's greats. His family, including his brother Mungo Park and his son Willie Park Jr., continued his golfing legacy, both winning Open Championships themselves.

Park's career influenced the popularization and professionalization of golf. His aggressive self-promotion and participation in high-profile matches improved press coverage and financial gains for golfers. He held the record for the most Open Championship wins until 1910.

Born: June 30, 1833
Birthplace: Wallyford, East Lothian
Star Sign: Cancer

Died: July 25, 1903 (aged 70)

Playing Career

  • 1860-10-17 1st British Open Men's Golf, Prestwick GC: Willie Park Sr. wins inaugural event by 2 strokes from fellow Scot Tom Morris Sr
  • 1861-09-26 British Open Men's Golf, Prestwick GC: Tom Morris Sr. beats Willie Park Sr. by 4 strokes
  • 1862-09-11 British Open Men's Golf, Prestwick GC: Tom Morris Sr retains his title; beats Willie Park Sr. by 3 strokes
  • 1863-09-18 British Open Men's Golf, Prestwick GC: Willie Park, Sr. wins his 2nd title; beats rival Tom Morris Sr. by 2 strokes
  • 1865-09-14 British Open Men's Golf, Prestwick GC: Scotsman Andrew Strath wins his first title; beats 2-time champion Willie Park Sr. by 2 strokes
  • 1866-09-13 British Open Men's Golf, Prestwick GC: Willie Park Sr. wins his 3rd title; beats his brother Davie Park by 2 strokes
  • 1867-09-26 British Open Men's Golf, Prestwick GC: Tom Morris Sr. wins 4th title; beats rival Willie Park Sr. by 2 strokes; at 46, Morris remains oldest Open champion
  • 1875-09-10 British Open Men's Golf, Prestwick GC: Willie Park Sr. wins his 4th title; beats fellow Scot Bob Martin by 2 strokes

Personal Life

  • 1860-03-29 Willie Park Sr. marries Susanna Law in Inveresk, Scotland
