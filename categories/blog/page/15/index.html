Will the Calm in Baghdad Last?

Aug 29, 2024 As the sun sets over the Jadriya bridge in central Baghdad , groups of young men jostle each other, laughing wildly on the sidewalk; their cars parked in a line that stretches from one side of the Tigris River to the other. In a country now in its fifth year of war, the sight may be unexpected. But perhaps more startling is what they’re drinking: beer. Local residents say that sort of activity hasn’t occurred here in public since the first year of the war — now looked upon nostalgically as the year before life really got bad.
