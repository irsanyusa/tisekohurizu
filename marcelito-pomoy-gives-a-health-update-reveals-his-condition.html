Marcelito Pomoy Gives a Health Update, Reveals His Condition

TALENTRECAP.COM Talent Got Talent

Photo by JC Olivera/FilmMagic

Reading Time: 2 minutes

The talented Filipino singer who won Pilipinas Got Talent in 2011 and competed on AGT: The Champions in 2020, Marcelito Pomoy, recently had fans worried about his health after a cryptic Instagram post. However, Pomoy has now given his fans a much-needed health update, assuring his fans that he’s going to be just fine.

Marcelito Pomoy Assures Fans That He’s Just Fine

In a new post, he revealed that he had undergone shockwave treatment for kidney stones, which were causing him discomfort. He expressed his appreciation for his fans’ support and well wishes.

The shockwave treatment that Pomoy underwent is a non-invasive procedure that uses high-energy sound waves to break up kidney stones into smaller pieces. Which can then be passed out of the body through urination.

Pomoy’s fans responded to his health update with an outpouring of support, leaving comments expressing relief and well wishes. Many fans shared their own experiences with kidney stones or offered advice on how to prevent them.

As of now, Pomoy is recovering and hoping to avoid further procedures. He expressed his appreciation for his fans’ support and well wishes, saying “Thank you so so much to all of you!” We wish him a speedy and complete recovery and look forward to hearing more from this talented performer in the future.

Marcelito Pomoy’s Musical Journey From Pilipinas Got Talent to AGT: The Champions

The talented Filipino singer shot to fame after winning the first season of Pilipinas Got Talent in 2011, capturing the hearts of audiences with his impressive vocal range and unique ability to sing in both tenor and soprano voices.

Pomoy’s success on Pilipinas Got Talent paved the way for even greater achievements, including a stint on the second season of AGT: The Champions in 2020. During his time on the show, Pomoy wowed the judges and viewers alike with his incredible performance of “Beauty and the Beast,” in which he seamlessly transitioned between the song’s male and female parts.

Despite finishing in fourth place, Pomoy left a lasting impression on the show and became a fan favorite, earning him a dedicated following on social media. His ability to captivate audiences with his singing and stage presence has made him a beloved performer not just in the Philippines but around the world.
