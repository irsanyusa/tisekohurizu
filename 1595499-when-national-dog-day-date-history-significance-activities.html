What day is National Dog Day?

National Dog Day, or International Dog Day in some countries, is a day to celebrate all dog breeds worldwide. People are encouraged to show love, kindness, and care to all canines and to own or adopt puppies in foster care.

When is National Dog Day?

National Dog Day is celebrated on 26 AugustAugust 26 every year. The date is special as it coincides with the date 10-year-old Colleen Paige's family adopted their first pup from a nearby dog shelter. National Dog Day activities

Dogs are great pets who sometimes become like family. Therefore, celebrating them on their special day is an excellent idea. Below is a list of fun activities you can do on National Dog Day 2014.

1. Throw a dog party

Just like humans, animals love a good party. Organise a bash and invite your friends and neighbours who have puppies. Ensure there are enough treats, food, and activities to do. You can also decorate the venue and give them gifts.

2. Bake homemade dog treats

If you love to bake, you can indulge in treat recipes for your pet. These may include cake, pancakes, and other foods your pet loves. Ensure they are not only sweet but healthy as well.

3. Sign up for dog training classes

These classes should be done alongside your pet. Together, you can learn from each other and better understand each other in the future. The classes can be as basic as rolling to more complex dog classes. This is one of the long-lasting gifts on this occasion.

4. Visit foster and volunteer with dogs

This is an excellent way to celebrate if you do not have dogs. You can sign up at a local shelter and foster a pet or volunteer to help on this day. You can do this for a day or foster for a while as you consider owning the pet long-term.

5. Create awareness

Another way to celebrate is to create an awareness campaign to promote the adoption of dogs. Promote the message of loving canines and allowing them to live an abuse-free life for all canines.

6. Go for a photo shoot with your pet

Look for a talented photographer to capture beautiful photos for your pup. They can either come home and take pictures there, or you can visit their studio with your pet. Have a change of clothes and carry accessories for your pup as well.

7. Organise a playday

Celebrate by going out with your adorable creature and other canines to play. You can take them to a park nearby or visit one of your friends' places. While you are there, organise special treats for the pups, such as snacks and food.

8. Spa day

Book a spa day with your canine friend and get a treat on this special day. Several places allow pets for a fee. Let them enjoy a good grooming session that includes cleaning, shampooing, and shaving.

9. Donate to a local shelter

This is a great way to celebrate all the dogs. You can check their websites for their needs, from food to treats to accessories. You can shop for these things and have them delivered or donate in cash.

10. Go for a swim with your pet

You can swim at a public pool if you and your pet are water lovers. Ask if dogs are allowed. You can also check out pools designated for pets or visit the nearby lake. Ensure that your dog has a water vest to avoid accidents.
